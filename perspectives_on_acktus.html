#### Motivation * I study fundamental principles of automated reasoning (using abstract argumentation as my "preferred" formal approach) * These principles have been introduced to analyze the behavior of decision-support and automation systems, *e.g.*: Gabbay's 1985 paper: "Theoretical foundations for non-monotonic reasoning in expert systems"
#### Example - Reference Independence in Decision Automation Systems * We want to determine the relevant citizenship (passports) of a client * Example: case handling of immigration or tax administration * We use decision management software (a real-world system) * The decision models can be deployed to high-scalability engines such as [jDMN](https://goldmansachs.github.io/jdmn/)
### Application Perspectives * Use my principle(s) to analyze real-world systems * Implement features that automate the analysis * Add new capabilities for dialogue reasoning, see: Kampik, Gabbay: "Towards DiArg: An Argumentation-based Dialogue Reasoning Engine"
